TACO SALAD

Taco salad American dish

Taco salad is a dish that originated in the United States. It is not a traditional
Mexican dish but was inspired by Mexican cuisine. The exact origin and creator of the
taco salad are not definitively known, but it is believed to have emerged in the mid-20th century.
It gained popularity as a Tex-Mex dish, which is a fusion of Mexican and American cuisines.
The combination of taco ingredients, such as seasoned meat, lettuce, tomatoes, cheese, and toppings,
served in a crispy tortilla bowl or over a bed of lettuce, became a popular variation of the traditional taco.
Over time, taco salad has evolved and is now enjoyed in various forms with a range of ingredients and dressings.

Ingredients

  • 2 tablespoons taco seasoning (from one 1-ounce package)
  • 2 tablespoons taco seasoning (from one 1-ounce package)
  • 1 teaspoon olive oil
  • 2/3 cup water
  • 5 loosely packed cups chopped romaine lettuce
  • 1 cup finely shredded Mexican blend cheese, or the cheese of your choice
  • 4 green onions, chopped
  • 1 1/2 cups cherry or grape tomatoes, halved
  • 1 (2.25-ounce) can sliced black olives, drained
  • 8 tostadas, lightly crushed
  • 4 tablespoons full fat sour cream
  • 1/2 cup salsa of your choice
  • 1 lime, quartered

Instructions

  1. Brown the meat: Place a medium skillet over medium-high heat. Add olive oil then beef into the pan
  2. Prep the salad ingredients:
  3. Sauce the meat: Once the ground beef has fully browned, turn the heat to medium and sprinkle the taco seasoning over it.
    Stir to coat meat, and then pour the water in the pan, stirring again to make sure there are no dry spots or clumps of seasoning.
  4. Assemble the salads:Remove beef from the heat and set aside but keep warm. Do not drain.
    Pasta bowls are ideal for these main dish salads, but any big plate or bowl will work. For each serving, layer as follows: 1 crushed tostada, 1 cup loosely packed lettuce,
    1/2 cup meat, 1/4 cup shredded cheese, 1/3 cup tomatoes, sprinkle of chopped green onion,
    2 tablespoons olives, and 1 more lightly crushed tostada. Garnish with 1 tablespoon sour
    cream and 2 tablespoons salsa. Serve with lime wedges on the side for squeezing.
